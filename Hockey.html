All you need to know about Hockey!

Hockey, specifically ice hockey, began to take shape in Canada in the late 19th century. While stick-and-ball games existed for centuries in Europe, the modern version of ice hockey was organized in Montreal in 1875 with the first indoor game. The sport grew quickly in colder regions and became known for its speed, physicality, and teamwork. In 1917, the National Hockey League (NHL) was formed, helping the sport expand across North America. Today, hockey is played worldwide and is celebrated for its fast-paced action, skillful skating, and passionate fan culture.

Hockey Rules:

  • Each team has 6 players on the ice at a time (3 forwards, 2 defensemen, and 1 goalie).
  • The main objective is to score goals by shooting the puck into the opponent’s net.
  • Players use hockey sticks to handle the puck; intentionally using hands to advance play is not allowed, except for the goalie.
  • A standard game is played in three 20-minute periods.
  • Offside rule: A player cannot enter the offensive zone before the puck crosses the blue line.
  • Icing: occurs when a player shoots the puck from behind the center line across the opponent’s goal line without it being touched.
  • Penalties: (tripping, checking from behind, slashing, etc.) send players to the penalty box, giving the other team a power play.
  • Face-offs: are used to restart play after stoppages.
